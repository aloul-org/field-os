# FieldOS AI — Advertising Video Prompts & Marketing Playbook

This file gives you (1) ready-to-use prompts for AI video generators (Sora, Runway,
Pika, Kling, Veo, HeyGen, Synthesia, Arcads, etc.), and (2) a grounded plan for how
to actually market FieldOS AI to win trade-business customers — honestly and
effectively.

> **Golden rule for trade marketing:** the buyer is a busy, sceptical, hands-on
> business owner who has been burned by "software that promised everything." Sell
> the *outcome* (more booked jobs, fewer missed calls, getting paid faster, knowing
> your real profit), never the feature list. Show the product doing one real thing,
> in their world, in under 10 seconds.

---

## Part 1 — How to advertise FieldOS AI (the right way)

### 1.1 Who you're actually talking to
- **Primary:** owner-operators and small-team bosses in plumbing, electrical, HVAC,
  roofing, landscaping, and general trades (1–20 staff). They run the business from
  a van and a phone.
- **Secondary:** office managers / dispatchers at 20–100-person firms who feel the
  pain of scheduling and chasing invoices.
- **What keeps them up at night:** missed calls = lost jobs; quotes taking too long;
  double-bookings; not knowing if a job actually made money; chasing late payments.

### 1.2 The messages that convert (lead with these)
1. **"Never miss another job."** The AI answers every call, even at 7pm on a
   Saturday, and books the lead.
2. **"Quote from a photo, in minutes."** Snap the boiler, get a priced quote.
3. **"Know which jobs actually make you money."** Real per-job profit, not guesses.
4. **"Get paid faster."** One-tap invoices and payment links.
5. **"It runs the boring stuff so you can do the work."** One platform, AI through
   all of it.

Pick **one** message per video. Don't cram.

### 1.3 Honest-marketing guardrails (protect the brand)
- **Don't fake the AI.** Show real interactions and real output. If a number is on
  screen, it should be a number the product could actually produce.
- **No invented statistics.** Avoid "increase revenue 300%!" unless you have a real,
  attributable case study with permission. Use *mechanisms* ("answers every call")
  not unprovable outcomes.
- **Show, don't claim.** A 6-second screen recording of a quote being generated beats
  any adjective.
- **Respect the trade.** No clueless-tradesperson clichés. The hero is competent and
  busy; the software just removes friction.
- **Disclose AI voices/avatars** where required by platform/region rules.
- **Money claims need proof.** "Get paid faster" is a mechanism (instant links);
  "earn £40k more" is a claim that needs a real testimonial.

### 1.4 Channel strategy
| Channel | Use it for | Format |
|---|---|---|
| **Meta (FB/IG) Reels** | Cold reach to owner-operators (they live on Facebook) | 9:16, 15–30s, captioned |
| **YouTube (pre-roll + Shorts)** | Intent ("job management software") + demos | 6s bumper + 60–90s demo |
| **TikTok** | "day in the life of a tradie" relatability | 9:16, native, fast |
| **Google Search** | High-intent keywords | Landing page, not video |
| **LinkedIn** | Larger firms / dispatchers | 60s problem→solution |
| **Trade forums / FB groups** | Trust via community (be a member, not a spammer) | Organic + case studies |

### 1.5 A simple funnel
1. **Hook ad (15s):** one pain, one outcome → "See how it works."
2. **Demo video (60–90s):** the core loop (call → quote → paid) on screen.
3. **Landing page:** free trial, no card; one testimonial; 60-second explainer.
4. **Retarget:** anyone who watched >50% gets the "get paid faster" / pricing ad.

### 1.6 What to measure
Thumb-stop rate (3s views), hook retention (watch past 5s), cost per trial start,
trial→paid conversion. Kill anything that doesn't beat your control on cost-per-trial.

## Part 2 — Video concepts (what to make first)

Make these in order; the first three are the highest ROI.

1. **"The 7pm call"** (hook ad, 15s) — emotional + the #1 pain (missed calls).
2. **"Photo to quote"** (demo, 20s) — the most magical, screen-recordable moment.
3. **"Did that job make money?"** (demo, 30s) — the differentiator vs. competitors.
4. **"A day in the field"** (PWA story, 30s) — technician app, offline, signature.
5. **"Brand film"** (60–90s) — the full loop, for the homepage + YouTube.

Each concept below has a **paste-ready generator prompt**. Keep brand consistency:

> **Brand kit (paste into any prompt):** Primary colour Signal Orange `#FF5A1F`;
> ink/near-black `#15181B`; warm off-white backgrounds `#FAF9F7`. Modern, confident,
> grounded, *not* glossy corporate SaaS. Real trade settings (vans, job sites,
> toolboxes), natural light, handheld energy. Clean sans-serif type. Logo: a bold
> "F" in orange. Tone: capable and calm, never gimmicky.

### Concept 1 — "The 7pm call" (15s hook ad, 9:16)

**Paste-ready prompt:**
```
A cinematic vertical (9:16) 15-second ad. Golden-hour interior of a cosy home;
a homeowner looks frustrated holding a phone that rings out — a plumbing leak
under the sink behind them. Hard cut to a confident plumber relaxing at a family
dinner, phone face-down on the table. Text overlay (clean bold sans-serif):
"Missed calls are missed jobs." Then the phone screen lights up showing a tidy
app notification: "New booked job — leak repair, tomorrow 9am." The plumber smiles,
keeps eating. End card on warm off-white #FAF9F7 with an orange "F" logo and text
"FieldOS AI — never miss another job." Natural light, shallow depth of field,
documentary feel, no cheesy stock-actor vibe. Signal orange #FF5A1F accents only.
```
**On-screen captions:** "It's 7pm." → "Your phone's ringing." → "FieldOS answers,
asks the right questions, and books the job." → "Never miss another job."

**Note:** for the UI shots, use a **real screen recording** of the estimate builder

## Part 4 — Production tips for credible AI video

- **Splice real screen recordings** of the actual app into generated footage for any
  UI moment. Generated UIs look "off" to the trained eye; real ones build trust.
- **Caption everything** — 80%+ of feed video is watched muted. Burn in captions.
- **First 3 seconds = the whole game.** Open on the pain or the payoff, never a logo.
- **One idea per video.** If you're explaining two features, make two videos.
- **Use real trades people** (or well-cast, non-glamorous actors) and real settings.
- **Vertical-first** for social; make a 16:9 cut for YouTube/website.
- **End card consistency:** same logo, same orange, same one-line promise every time.
- **Localise** for German-speaking markets (the product is bilingual) — translate
  captions/VO, keep the brand kit identical.

| Week | Focus |
|---|---|
| **1** | Record 3–4 real screen captures (call→lead, photo→quote, profit dashboard, tech app). Generate Concepts 1–3. Build the landing page + free-trial flow. |
| **2** | Launch Concept 1 (hook) + Concept 2 (demo) on Meta Reels with a small budget across 3–4 audiences (plumbers, electricians, HVAC, general). Measure cost-per-trial. |
| **3** | Cut the winners; add Concept 3 (profit) and retargeting. Start YouTube demo + 6s bumper on intent keywords. Post organically in 2–3 trade FB groups (value, not spam). |
| **4** | Double down on the lowest cost-per-trial creative. Collect first real testimonials; turn one into a case-study video. Plan the German-market cut. |
