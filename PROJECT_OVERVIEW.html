Project Overview

FieldOS AI

An AI operating system for field-service companies — plumbers, electricians, HVAC, and trades. It doesn't just digitise the paperwork; it runs the business: answers the phone, books the job, schedules the technician, writes the estimate, chases the invoice, and tells the owner why profit moved last month.

What it is, in one paragraph

Most field-service tools (Jobber, Housecall Pro, ServiceTitan) are digital filing cabinets — they store your jobs and invoices. FieldOS AI is built around an AI layer that runs through every module. A missed call becomes a scored lead with a written summary. A photo of a broken boiler becomes a priced quote. A spoken note from the van becomes a clean customer-ready job report. A simple question — "why was profit down in March?" — becomes a real answer grounded in your own numbers. It scales from a solo operator to a 100-person team, hiding the multi-technician complexity when you don't need it.

Three surfaces, one brand

The same design language and data, presented for three very different users.

Office App

The command centre

Desktop-first dashboards for owners, dispatchers and estimators: leads, scheduling, estimates, invoicing, finance, coaching. Dense, information-rich. Opens straight into the AI estimate builder — the headline feature is the home page, pinned to the top of the sidebar — so the most valuable action is one screen, not a menu hunt away.

Technician PWA

The field app

Installable, dark-mode, mobile-only-in-practice. Big tap targets for gloved hands, voice & photo over typing, works offline in a basement. Lives at /tech.

Customer surfaces

The clean decision

No-login, tokenised pages: accept a quote, pay an invoice, view the client portal. Calm and single-purpose — get one clean "yes" from someone who didn't ask to use software.

How it works — the core revenue loop

Every feature hangs off this end-to-end flow. Each arrow is automated or AI-assisted.

📞 Lead captured→ 🤖 Scored & routed→ 📝 AI estimate→ ✅ Customer accepts→ 🗓️ Job scheduled→ 🔧 Technician completes→ 💷 Invoice paid→ 📊 Profit calculated→ ⭐ Review & renewal

A phone call to the AI receptionist creates a lead; Claude scores it hot/warm/cold and the owner is notified. One click turns it into an estimate — drafted from text, voice, or a photo, with every price recomputed server-side (the AI never does the arithmetic). The customer opens a clean public link and accepts, which auto-creates a job. The dispatcher schedules it (with an AI "best technician & slot" suggestion and route optimisation); the technician runs it from their phone — checklist, photos, a spoken report transcribed and cleaned by AI, and a finger-signature. That flips the job to complete and notifies the office to invoice. When the invoice is paid, FieldOS computes the job's profit (revenue − labour − materials − overhead) and feeds the finance dashboard and AI CFO. Finally it asks for a review and sets a renewal reminder.

The modules

1 · CRM & lead capture built

Multi-channel inbox

Leads from the AI phone receptionist, inbound WhatsApp/SMS, an embeddable website widget, and manual entry — all in one scored inbox.

AI voice receptionist AI

Twilio Voice answers, runs a slot-filling conversation (issue → address → urgency), summarises the call, and creates a scored lead.

Lead scoring AI

Every lead is classified hot / warm / cold with a one-line reason. Hot & warm interrupt the owner; cold ones batch into the daily digest.

Website widget

A drop-in <script> that puts a "Get a quote" button on any site, with a honeypot and rate limiting.

2 · AI estimating & quotes built

Speak / Type / Photo AI

Describe the job three ways. Claude extracts structured line items; the server recomputes every subtotal, VAT and total — the model is never trusted with money.

Historical pricing anchors AI

The five most recent accepted quotes in the same trade are injected as anchors so prices stay consistent with what the business actually charges.

Win-probability score AI

Before sending, a green/amber/red badge estimates the chance of acceptance from real win-rate stats — visible to the contractor only.

Public acceptance

A clean tokenised page to accept or decline; accepting auto-creates the job and notifies the office.

Try-before-signup demo AI

The marketing home page runs the real estimating engine for anonymous visitors (rate-limited per IP, with a canned fallback). Describe a job, get an itemised quote, then sign up to save & send it.

3 · Scheduling & dispatch built

Live dispatch board

Per-technician lanes by day, updating in real time via Supabase Realtime — no refresh needed.

AI auto-scheduler AI

A transparent scoring formula (skills + travel time + overtime risk) suggests the best technician and slot. It suggests; a human always confirms.

Route optimisation

Google Routes reorders a technician's day for the shortest total drive, with drive-time labels between stops.

Emergency fit-in

Slots a same-day emergency by proposing the safest lower-priority job to bump to tomorrow — never auto-moving another customer silently.

4 · Technician PWA built

Today & job detail

Today's stops as big cards with a single lifecycle button (On my way → Arrived → Complete) and tap-to-navigate addresses.

Photos, voice reports & signature AI

Camera photos by type, a spoken report transcribed by Whisper and cleaned by Claude, and a full-screen finger-signature to close the job.

Offline-first

Today's jobs viewable with no signal; photo/voice uploads queue locally and retry automatically when connectivity returns.

Trade copilot AI

A scoped on-the-job assistant for trade questions (with photo troubleshooting) — deliberately blind to pricing and customer financials.

5 · Invoicing & payments built

One-click invoicing

Create an invoice from a completed job, pulling line items from the estimate; generate a PDF from the shared template.

Stripe billing + Connect

Subscriptions for FieldOS itself, plus Stripe Connect payment links for customer invoices (a transparent 0.5% platform fee).

6 · Materials built

Inventory & stock

Stock-level bars that flag low items against a reorder threshold.

Supplier requests AI

Drafts a clean restock request and emails the supplier — or gives a copyable summary. AI writes a weekly consumption-forecast summary (the math is done in code).

7 · Finance intelligence built

Profitability

Per-job profit (revenue − labour − materials − overhead) rolled up by job, technician, service line and customer.

Revenue forecast

A trailing moving-average with seasonal adjustment — real statistics in code; Claude only writes the plain-English narrative.

AI CFO AI

Ask financial questions in plain language. Claude uses a fixed set of safe, read-only, company-scoped data tools — no free-form SQL, no hallucinated numbers.

AI Business Coach AI

The same tool-calling engine, broadened to operations. Suggested questions surface only when the data is genuinely notable (e.g. a real win-rate drop).

8 · Retention built

Reviews & recovery

Auto-ask happy customers for a Google review; "log a bad review" flags the customer and drafts a personal recovery message before it's posted publicly.

Renewals & team

Recurring-service reminders (boiler service, gas safety) on a daily cron, plus full team management with role-based access enforcement.

The AI layer

Deliberate, safe, and never trusted with things code should do.

PrincipleHow it's applied
Right model for the jobclaude-sonnet for live, user-facing work (estimating, CFO, coach); claude-haiku for high-volume background tasks (lead scoring, summaries, nudges).
Never trust the LLM with money or mathEvery total, VAT figure, profit number and forecast is computed in TypeScript. The model extracts and explains; code calculates.
Tool-calling, not SQL generationThe CFO & Coach call a fixed set of parameterised, company-scoped read-only functions — eliminating injection risk and cross-tenant leakage.
Validate every responseAI JSON is parsed and re-validated with Zod before it touches the database.
Graceful degradationIf an AI/integration key is missing, the feature shows a friendly fallback (e.g. type a report instead of recording) rather than breaking.

Architecture & tech stack

Framework & UI
  • Next.js 14 (App Router, Server Components, Server Actions)
  • TypeScript (strict), Tailwind CSS, shadcn/ui (Radix)
  • next-intl (English + German, cookie-driven)
  • next-pwa for the installable, offline technician app
Data & backend
  • Supabase: Postgres, Auth, Storage, Realtime
  • Row Level Security on every table — the real tenant boundary
  • Zod validation on every input and AI response
  • 15 ordered SQL migrations; hand-maintained typed schema
AI & integrations
  • Anthropic Claude — reasoning, extraction, chat, vision
  • OpenAI Whisper — voice transcription
  • Twilio — voice receptionist, SMS/WhatsApp
  • Stripe — billing + Connect payments
  • Google Maps/Routes, Resend (email)
Security model
  • RLS scopes all data to the user's company
  • Role-based access (owner, admin, dispatcher, estimator, technician, viewer)
  • Service-role key used only server-side (webhooks, cron)
  • Public flows use unguessable tokens + signed webhooks
One design language across all three surfaces. Signal-orange primary, cool-graphite ink, warm paper neutrals; a "job ticket" card motif and a "route line" device tie the brand to the real-world paperwork it replaces. The technician app is the same tokens in dark mode with bigger touch targets.
