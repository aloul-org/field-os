1How to read this

FieldOS AI is a multi-tenant SaaS, so cost falls into three buckets:

  • AI Per-operation — pay-per-token LLM and transcription calls. Scales directly with how much each customer uses the AI features.
  • Platform Fixed monthly — Supabase + Vercel + email, paid once for the whole platform regardless of tenant count (until you outgrow a tier).
  • Usage Metered third-party — Twilio voice/SMS/WhatsApp and Google Maps, billed per minute/message/request and attributable to individual tenants.

The AI layer is provider-switchable (AI_PROVIDER=anthropic|openai), so every AI line shows both. The app splits work across a live model (user-facing: estimating, copilot, Coach) and a cheaper background model (high-volume: lead scoring, forecasts, summaries) — see lib/ai/anthropic.ts / lib/ai/openai.ts.

Per-tenant metered services

  • Voice receptionist is the heaviest metered feature: each inbound call ≈ voice minutes ($0.0085/min) ＋ speech recognition (~$0.04/min). A 3-minute call ≈ $0.15, before the AI summary.
  • Phone number: $1.15/month per tenant that provisions one.
  • SMS / WhatsApp lead capture & renewals: roughly $0.01–0.05 per message all-in (carrier/Meta fees included).
  • Google Maps: auto-scheduling travel times & route optimisation; plus the new daily review poll (/api/cron/review-poll) which calls Places Details once per tenant per day — ~30 calls/tenant/month. The reviews field sits on Google's pricier Places SKU (~$20–25/1k), but at one call/day a tenant is well inside the 1,000/mo free Enterprise allowance unless you have 30+ active tenants.
  • Mapbox: map rendering; 50k free loads/mo covers everything but high-traffic deployments.

7The Stripe caveat

Stripe's 2.9% + $0.30 per charge is the largest dollar figure in the whole system, but it is not an operating cost of running FieldOS AI — it's a transaction fee on the tenant's own customer payments, deducted from their revenue. On the Stripe Connect Standard model used here, the platform adds no extra per-account or per-payout fee; the connected business pays Stripe directly.

So a plumber processing $50,000/month through invoices pays ~$1,450 in Stripe fees on their income — that scales with their turnover, not with your hosting bill, and should be modelled as a pass-through, never lumped into platform COGS.

8Cost levers, biggest first

  1. Voice receptionist minutes — by far the largest per-tenant variable. Shorter call flows and per-tenant minute caps have the most impact.
  2. AI Coach round count — MAX_TOOL_ROUNDS=5. Lowering it, or capping turns/day per plan tier, bounds the priciest AI action.
  3. Prompt caching / batch API — up to -90%/-50% on the high-volume background calls; not yet enabled.
  4. Provider choice — Anthropic is ~half the live-model cost today.
  5. Platform tiers — fixed and shared; only matters at scale (Supabase compute, Vercel bandwidth/invocation overages).
  6. Google review poll cadence — daily is cheap; only watch it once you have dozens of tenants pushing Places calls past the free Enterprise allowance.
