1Getting started

Create your account, set up your business once, and you're ready to take work.

Create an account & log in

/register · /login

Start a free trial with your email, or use Continue with Google if your team enabled it. Forgot your password? Use /login/reset to get a reset link by email.

The setup wizard

/onboarding

A short, six-step wizard the first time you sign in. Work through it in order:

  • Company — your business name, contact details and language (English/German).
  • Trade — your main trade(s); this tunes the AI's estimating and lead scoring.
  • Pricing defaults — hourly rate, call-out fee, VAT and payment terms. These pre-fill every quote and invoice.
  • Team — invite the people who'll use FieldOS (you can skip and do this later).
  • Plan — pick a subscription (Starter / Growth / Pro).
  • Integrations — optionally connect phone, payments and email now, or later from Settings.
TipAnything you skip here you can finish later in Settings. Nothing is locked in.

The welcome tour

The first time you reach the dashboard, a guided tour pops up and walks you through each area in about a minute. Re-run it any time from your profile menu (top-right) → Take the tour.

2Finding your way around

Three ways to move through the app — pick whichever is fastest for you.

The command palette fastest

Press Ctrl + K  (or ⌘ + K on a Mac)

A search box opens from anywhere. Start typing to jump to any page or run a quick action like “New estimate” or “Ask the AI Coach”. Use the arrow keys to choose and Enter to go; Esc closes it. It only ever shows what your role can access.

The sidebar

On the left (desktop) your sections sit as stops on a route line — the glowing orange stop is the page you're on. On phones and tablets the same navigation lives in the bottom bar, with a More button for the rest.

The top bar

Holds the search box (opens the command palette), the notifications bell, and your profile menu — where you switch language (English ⇄ German), replay the tour, open this manual, and log out.

3Dashboard

Your daily overview

/dashboard

Everyone with office access

What it's for: a glance at how the business is doing today — revenue this month, jobs booked today, estimates awaiting a reply, and money outstanding.

How to use it: the Needs attention card lists the things worth acting on right now — hot leads, emergency jobs with no slot yet, and overdue invoices. Click any row to jump straight to it.

4Leads

Leads inbox

/leads

Owner, Admin, Dispatcher, Estimator

What it's for: every new enquiry in one place — from your website widget, the phone receptionist, WhatsApp and SMS, or added by hand.

How to use it: each lead is scored hot / warm / cold by the AI (the coloured rail on the left flags the hot ones). Filter with the tabs along the top, or click Add lead to enter one manually.

Lead detail

/leads/[id]

How to use it: read the enquiry and the AI's reasoning, then assign it to a technician (FieldOS suggests the best skill match), mark it contacted / lost / converted, or click through to create an estimate pre-filled from the lead's details.

5Calls

Call log

/calls · /calls/[id]

Owner, Admin, Dispatcher

What it's for: a record of calls handled by the AI voice receptionist, each with the caller's details, what they wanted, and an AI summary.

How to use it: open a call to read the full summary and the captured issue/address/urgency. Calls that look like real work become leads automatically. Requires Twilio

6Schedule & dispatch

Dispatch board

/schedule

Owner, Admin, Dispatcher

What it's for: plan the day across your whole team — one lane per technician, with a panel of jobs still waiting for a slot.

How to use it:

  • Use the date arrows to move between days.
  • Schedule a waiting job into a slot, or let “Suggest best technician & slot” rank your team by skills, travel time and overtime. AI
  • For an emergency with no room, “Fit in today” proposes bumping the safest lower-priority job to tomorrow — you confirm before anything moves.
  • When a technician has three or more stops, Optimise route reorders them for the least driving and shows drive-times. Needs Google Maps key
  • The board updates live as the team works — no need to refresh.

7Jobs

Jobs list

/jobs

Owner, Admin, Dispatcher

What it's for: all work in progress and its status, from unscheduled through to completed and invoiced.

Job detail

/jobs/[id]

How to use it: see the customer, appointments, the technician's photos, the report and any captured signature. Add or reschedule appointments here. Once a job is completed or invoiced, the follow-up card lets you request a review or set up a maintenance renewal in one click.

8Estimates

Estimates list

/estimates

Owner, Admin, Estimator

What it's for: every quote you've drafted or sent, with its status and win probability.

Build an estimate

/estimates/new

How to use it: three ways to start, all on one screen:

  • Type a description of the work,
  • Photo — upload pictures and let the AI read what's needed,
  • Speak — describe the job out loud.

The AI drafts itemised lines and a price using your rates and past jobs as anchors. You always review and edit before sending — and the totals are recalculated by FieldOS, never guessed by the AI. AI Requires Anthropic

Estimate detail

/estimates/[id]

How to use it: fine-tune line items, then send the quote. The customer gets a link to accept online; when they do, a job is created automatically.

9Invoices

Invoices list

/invoices

Owner, Admin (Viewer: read-only)

What it's for: everything you've billed and what's still owed (sent, paid, overdue).

Invoice detail

/invoices/[id]

How to use it: raise an invoice from a job or estimate, edit it, then send it with a payment link. Mark it paid when money arrives (or it's marked automatically when paid by card). The moment it's paid, the job's real profit is calculated. Card payments need Stripe

10Customers

Customer list & detail

/customers · /customers/[id]

Owner, Admin, Dispatcher, Estimator

What it's for: your address book — contact details, their properties, and a full history of leads, quotes, jobs and invoices.

How to use it: open a customer to add a property, see everything you've done for them, and start a new quote or job from their record.

11Materials

Materials & stock

/materials

Owner, Admin

What it's for: track parts and stock levels, with a banner when something's running low.

How to use it: add materials and suppliers, set stock levels, and use Restock to draft a supplier request (emailed for you, or copy the text). A consumption forecast estimates when you'll run out. AI summary

12Finance & AI CFO

Finance dashboard

/finance

Owner, Admin (Viewer: read-only)

What it's for: your real profit — not just turnover. See profitability broken down by job, technician, service and customer, plus a forecast of next month's revenue.

How to use it: switch tabs to change the breakdown. Profit per job uses labour (hours × rate), materials, and a fair share of overheads. The forecast uses your own trend and seasonality; the AI only writes the plain-English summary. AI narrative

AI CFO

/finance/ai-cfo

What it's for: ask money questions in plain English — “Which service makes the best margin?”, “How did revenue move this quarter?”

How to use it: type a question. The assistant pulls the real figures from your finance data to answer; it never invents numbers. AI

13AI Business Coach

Coach

/coach

Owner, Admin (Viewer: read-only)

What it's for: a wider business advisor — leads, conversion, win rates and profit — that answers from your own data.

How to use it: start with a suggested question, or ask your own. Smart chips appear only when something's worth flagging (e.g. a real drop in win rate). Past conversations are saved in the rail on the left. AI

14Reviews

Reviews & recovery

/reviews

Owner, Admin

What it's for: turn happy customers into public reviews, and catch unhappy ones privately first.

How to use it: send a review request after a job (it tries WhatsApp, then SMS, then email). If feedback is poor, log a bad review to flag the customer, alert the office, and get a suggested outreach message — so you fix it before it goes public. Messaging needs Twilio

15Team

Team management

/team

Owner, Admin only

What it's for: the people in your company and what they can do.

How to use it: invite members by email, set each person's role (which decides what they can see and change), and deactivate anyone who's left. The owner account is protected and can't be locked out.

16Settings

Set your business up once; everything else inherits from here.

Company & defaults

/settings

How to use it: business name, contact details, branding, language, VAT, payment terms, hourly rate, call-out fee and monthly overhead. These feed quotes, invoices and the profit calculations.

Billing

/settings/billing

Owner only

Your FieldOS subscription plan and payment details. Stripe

Lead capture (website widget)

/settings/lead-capture

Turn on the embeddable “Get a quote” widget for your website and copy the snippet. Submissions arrive in your Leads inbox, scored automatically.

Voice receptionist

/settings/voice-receptionist

Set the greeting and the inbound phone number the AI receptionist answers on. Twilio

Integrations & notifications

/settings/integrations · /settings/notifications

Connect optional services (payments, messaging, maps, email) and choose what you get notified about. Every integration is optional — without it, the matching feature simply stays quiet rather than breaking.

17Technician app

A separate, phone-first app for the field — dark, big buttons, works with no signal. Technicians are taken here automatically when they log in.

Today

/tech/today

How to use it: the day's jobs in order. Tap the big status button to move a job along — scheduled → on my way → in progress → complete. Optionally turn on location sharing so the office can see you're en route.

Job detail

/tech/jobs/[id]

How to use it: work the checklist, snap before/after photos, record a quick voice report (transcribed and tidied for you), and capture the customer's signature to finish. No signal? Photos and reports queue up and upload themselves when you're back online.

Copilot

/tech/copilot

How to use it: a job-aware assistant for on-site questions — “how do I bleed this type of radiator?” AI

Profile & install

/tech/profile

How to use it: your details and log out. Install it like an app via your browser's “Add to Home Screen”, so it opens full-screen and loads instantly.

18What your customers see

Clean, no-login pages that ask for one simple decision. You don't manage these directly — they're the links you send.

Accept a quote

/quote/[token]

When you send an estimate, the customer opens a tidy page showing the quote and a single Accept button. Accepting creates the job for you automatically.

Pay an invoice

/invoice/[token]

The invoice with one Pay now button. Paid invoices update in FieldOS and trigger the profit calculation. Stripe

19Roles & who sees what

Each team member has a role. It controls which sections appear for them and whether they can make changes.

Owner

Everything, including billing. Can't be deactivated.

Admin

Everything except billing.

Dispatcher

Dashboard, schedule, jobs, leads, calls and customers — the day-to-day operations.

Estimator

Dashboard, estimates, customers and leads — focused on winning work.

Technician

The technician app only (no office screens).

Viewer

Read-only across the office app; can't change anything or manage the team/billing.

Sensitive areasFinance, the AI CFO and the Coach are limited to Owner and Admin (and read-only Viewer). The command palette and sidebar automatically hide anything your role can't open.
